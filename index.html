02 / The Model

The Stakeholder Model

A strategic business model that gives leaders a practical way to grow and develop employees or commissioned individuals while transforming them into true stakeholders.

This model combines role-specific career paths, systematic performance growth, and team-based collaboration strategies into one repeatable system.

Salaried and commissioned: no matter how someone is paid, the fundamentals are the same. Skill, experience, and collaboration drive long-term growth.

A coaching organization: this approach helps supervisors coach performance in a consistent way, creating a culture of ongoing development and accountability.

06 / Goal-Setting System

Six-Month Segment Goals

Clear goals are set for each six-month segment with outcomes defined so that what winning looks like is easy to see.

Month 1
Goals set for all three focus areas in alignment with company priorities.
Months 2-5
Monthly check-ins with self-assessment and supervisor coaching.
Month 6
Formal review with compensation decision, where appropriate.
Repeat
New goals, new segment, continuous growth.

Built-in succession planning: the cadence naturally creates matriculation pathways and prepares people for the next level.

08 / Review System

Monthly Check-ins, Not Annual Surprises

Each month, the employee or commissioned individual brings a self-evaluation to the supervisor, rating progress toward total completion of each goal on a 1-10 scale.

The supervisor affirms or gently redirects, then asks: "How can I support your progress this month?"

Key Insight: it is easier to gently correct monthly than to wait for a once-a-year conversation. Most people underestimate progress, so supervisors often end up encouraging and building them up.

✓
Self-Assessment Benefits
Empowerment, context and fairness, administrative easement, full support, no surprises.
⟳
Why Six-Month Sprints?
A series of sprints is easier to buy into than a long marathon, with flexibility to pivot in a changing market.
★
The Promise
When monthly check-ins are handled with care and consistency, the formal review produces zero surprises.
09 / Compensation Increase

Performance Drives Compensation

The model includes compensation increases tied to objective performance outcomes. Whether someone is hourly, salaried, or commissioned, long-term earning growth follows the same fundamentals: skill set, experience, and sound judgment applied to business outcomes.

Compensation increase logic: each role has a defined increase opportunity per segment. Earned increase is determined by documented goal achievement against agreed standards.

Compensation Formula
Earned Increase = Potential Increase × Goal Achievement
Example: if potential increase is 4.0% and goal achievement is 85%, earned increase equals 3.4%.

Commissioned individuals: staying on track to hit goals and receiving coaching ensures increasing skill, experience, and collaboration translate into stronger earning power over time.

Market-Based Strategy
Set target market percentile strategy and use objective external compensation data sources for calibration.
Getting Back on Track
If someone falls short of 100%, expectations stay clear. Strong performance in the next segment puts them back on track.
The Bottom Line
The system gives people ownership of career trajectory and value growth, regardless of compensation type.
